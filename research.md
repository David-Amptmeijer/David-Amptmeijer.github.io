# Research

My research focuses on mercury cycling in aquatic systems, with an emphasis on the processes that transform inorganic mercury into methylmercury (MeHg) and the biological mechanisms that regulate its bioaccumulation in marine and freshwater food webs.

---

## Themes

### **1. Biological controls on MeHg formation**
- DOM consumption  
- Microbial demethylation  
- Food web structure and trophic dynamics  

### **2. Bioaccumulation modelling**
- Benthic exposure pathways  
- Feeding strategy as a driver of Hg uptake  
- Bioconcentration dynamics in high‑trophic‑level fish

### **3. Ocean–atmosphere interactions**
- Air–sea Hg exchange  
- Seasonal drivers of Hg speciation  
- Coupled hydrodynamic–biogeochemical modelling

### **4. Modeling tool development**
- Integration of MeHg pathways into ecosystem models  
- Advancing pelagic–benthic coupling  
- Harmonization of multi-model Hg approaches

---

## Ongoing Interests
- 3‑D Hg modeling in lakes via FABM–GOTM  
- Ecosystem drivers of nonlinear MeHg responses  
- Improving mechanistic representation of benthic processes
